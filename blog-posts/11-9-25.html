Getting l33t in the year of our Lord 2025

11/9/25

My roomate came into my room, as he often does, and struck up a conversation about the idea of information overload, his area of research for his Science Technnology & Society degree. And we got to talking about it, the concept of how throughout the middle ages, people thought there were too many books.

Especially with the introduction of the printing press, there was an influx of writing on every topic. The idea of the renaissance man pushed intellectuals to read everything, develop novel systems of processing and accessing (mentally and physically) that knowledge. But the torrent of writing doesn't stop, and people fell behind, experienced information overload.

And so our conversation moved to the modern day, specifically in the field of computer science. How can we as programmers feel anything other than overwhelmed?

Looking at our fore-fathers, the punch-card programmers, the computer enthusiasts, the start of silicon valley, how did they do it? How did they understand their field? How did they become masters of their domain without google, without stack overflow, without anything else?

The answer that we settled on was that their focus was narrow. They stuck to one thing and organically developed their skills. Or maybe thats was our mythologized conception of them. Regardless, the access to information was significantlly limited back then.

I heard in one of my classes that computer geeks in the early days met up and just tried to one up each other. They got their hands on the best consumer grade parts, in magazines or from word of mouth. Then they just got together and showed off their skills. What programs they had designed. What hardware they got running, etc.

Today, programmers (especially students and beginners) are thrust into information overload; We have a paralyzing amount of information at our hands. There are million pathways ahead of us: what do you want to do? Oh web dev? What framework? What language? Are you fullstack?

So what can you do? Henry David Thoreau says "let your affairs be as two or three, and not a hundred or a thousand; instead of a million count half a dozen". But my brain has a bad habit of turning me into renaissance man; I want to do it all: writing, drawing, music, electrical engineering, backend, frontend, AI engineering, etc. etc. etc. But a choice has to be made.

So I am setting out to find out what that is. I do enjoy a lot of things that I want to do. I like web dev. I like AI/ML work. I like embeded systems/systems design. But I want to chose one. Which will, ironically, look a whole lot like the same scattered effort in the comming weeks or months. I'll play around until I settle on what it is. All this information doesn't have to be a bad thing, I can use to find that one rabit hole to throw myself down. Wish me luck!
