On Drawing Poorly

2/2/26

At a pace somewhere in between the tide and glacial drift, I have decided to try my hand at art again.

It's a 2 year cycle where I pick up ye olde sketchbook, draw every night for a couple weeks until I forget for a couple nights and it fades from my mind. And during those weeks, I'm drawing circles and lines, trying to get "the basics" down. It's drawing exercises and youtube tutorials all the way down, like there's some underlying process that I must master before I even attempt to make one full art.

My friend gave me great advice - "treat your early art like its worth nothing because it is." - but my ego is too damn wrapped up in my efforts and their sad, sad fruits. How can I ignore the effort I put into something? How can I ignore when it looks like shit? How am I supposed to be proud of something so imperfect?

"Learn how to be bad at something" is another piece of advice that gets slung around in art circles. It takes 10,000 hours to master a skill, so of course the shit you make is going to suck. But actually grokking that? Using it? Man it just sucks.

I saw this humorous Instagram Reel about how everyone is either in camp Voltaire or camp Rousseau, either ruled by logic or emotion. I think I've spent too damn long in front of screen, programming, gaming, learning rules and theorems not to be in team Rousseau. Which leaves me bereft when I actually try to create emotional art.

So I guess all I can do is try my damndest and suck shit at drawing for a while. Really on this pass I can feel myself absorbing that I do suck at drawing, but I am getting better. What I really need to learn now is consistency, but I've kept it up so far! I guess I'll make another post when im brave enough to share my bad art :-)
